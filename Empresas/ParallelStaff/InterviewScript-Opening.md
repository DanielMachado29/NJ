# Interview Script — Opening & Tell Me About Yourself

**Role:** Fullstack .NET Developer (LATAM Remote)  
**Format:** Video call · ~45 min · English  
**How to use:** Read aloud. Pause when you see **Yareli** — answer in your head or out loud before reading **You**.

---

## Strategy (before you introduce yourself)

Ask why the role is open **right after** the interviewer explains the interview flow — **before** "Tell me about yourself."

| If they say… | Emphasize in your intro |
|--------------|-------------------------|
| Team growth / new demand | Delivery speed, APIs, end-to-end features |
| Backfill | Fast ramp-up, documentation, continuity |
| Legacy (.NET Framework, jQuery) | SERGET migration, strangler fig pattern |
| Production fires | RabbitMQ, Redis, root-cause fixes, support partnership |
| APIs / integrations | REST, JWT, third-party integrations (ATIVA, GOL) |

**Pivot line after they answer:**  
*"That's really helpful — a lot of that lines up with what I've been doing. Let me walk you through my background with that in mind."*

## 3. Interview overview

### Yareli
Alright, let me give you a quick overview of how we'll run this. I'll start with a few questions about your background, then we'll go into some technical topics around .NET and full-stack work, and at the end you'll have time to ask me questions. Sound good?

---

## 4. Why is the role open? *(ask before introducing yourself)*

### You
Sounds great. Before I introduce myself — could you tell me why this role is open? Is it team growth, a backfill, or a specific need on the product side?

### Yareli *(example answer — adapt to what you hear in the real interview)*
Good question. It's mostly team growth — we're expanding the engineering team — but there's a specific need behind it. A lot of our day-to-day is maintaining existing .NET applications, some still on Framework with jQuery on the frontend, plus building and integrating APIs. We also need someone comfortable jumping on production issues with the support team when things get urgent. So it's not just greenfield work — it's a mix of legacy, APIs, and keeping things stable in production.

### You
That's really helpful — thank you. A lot of that lines up with what I've been doing, so let me walk you through my background with that in mind.

### Yareli
Perfect. Go ahead.

---

## 5. Tell me about yourself

### You

Sure. I'm a Full Stack Developer with a little over five years of experience, mostly focused on C# and the .NET ecosystem. I have a Bachelor's in Computer Science from UFJF here in Brazil.

Most of my career has been in enterprise environments — urban mobility, aviation, and legal tech — high-volume systems with complex business rules that need to stay reliable in production.

**Right now at SERGET**, I build and maintain REST APIs — JWT auth, profile-based authorization, centralized error handling. A big part of my work is modernizing legacy modules: migrating from .NET Framework to .NET 6, plus Redis caching, RabbitMQ, and background workers for heavy routines.

**Before that at CS Global IT**, I led backend work for GOL Airlines — .NET 8, Oracle, EF Core, Dapper, and integrations between internal systems. I also built document pipelines with OCR and AI to extract financial data from PDFs.

**On the side**, I'm building a legal process update chatbot — .NET 8, Docker, CI/CD — using AI-assisted tools like Cursor to move faster.

So — full stack, but my sweet spot is **backend and APIs**, especially legacy code, integrations, and performance challenges. *(← tailor this last line to what Yareli just said)*

### Yareli
Nice. You mentioned legacy modernization a couple of times. That's actually a big part of what we do here. We'll dig into that in a bit.
